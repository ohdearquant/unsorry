Added a superseded-PR janitor (`tools/repo/superseded_prs.py` + `superseded-pr-janitor.yml`) that auto-closes coordination/prove PRs whose action `main` has already satisfied — duplicate decompositions (first-merge-wins, the losers conflict), stacked `unblock(G)` PRs once G is no longer blocked, and `prove(G)` PRs once G is proved/archived. Such duplicates can never merge or are no-ops yet consume Gate A runs, the submission-governor in-flight budget, reviewer attention and queue space — left unmanaged they fill the queue. The janitor closes only on a positive already-satisfied signal (conservative: unknown goals / unrecognised titles / still-pending actions are left alone), runs event-driven on Gate A completion (a merge is what newly-supersedes competitors) plus an hourly backstop, and uses the default `GITHUB_TOKEN` (closes PRs, opens nothing). Pure predicates (`parse_pr_action`, `decomposed_goals`, `goal_statuses`, `is_superseded`) are unit-tested.
